Week 2 begins. The striker mentality starts now.

🌅 Morning · 55–60 min

EXPLOSIVE POWER + ADVANCED MOBILITY

Week 2 conditioning is harder. Longer sets, shorter rest, advanced movements.

Explosive Warm-Up — 8 min

1
A-Skip Drill 3×30 sec
Drive knee up, skip forward. This is the same warm-up used by Olympic sprinters. Activates hip flexors and improves stride mechanics — directly increases your sprint speed.
2
B-Skip + Leg Extension 3×20
A-skip but extend the raised leg forward at the top. Hamstring activation at speed. Prevents pulls and builds stride extension.
3
Explosive Hip Rotations (standing) 20 each direction
Arms extended, rapidly rotate hips side to side. Warms up rotation muscles used in shots, turns, and crosses.

Explosive Conditioning — 32 min

A
Depth Drop to Jump 4×8 reps · 75s rest
Step off a 20-30cm surface, land on both feet immediately jump as high as possible. This is reactive strength training — teaches your muscles to absorb and redirect force instantly. Directly improves your heading jump and explosive first step.
B
Pistol Squat Negatives 🦶 4×6 each leg · 5s down · start LEFT
Single leg squat, go down very slowly (5 seconds), use other foot to help on the way up if needed. The most powerful single-leg exercise. Builds the quad and glute strength of your weak leg to match your strong leg.
C
Explosive Lateral Bound 🦶 4×8 bounds each direction · land LEFT first
Single leg bound sideways as far as possible, stick the landing on one foot. Left foot landing = balance and strength work. Direct transfer to Doku's lateral cuts. Stick landing for 2 seconds before next bound.
D
Tuck Jumps 3×10 · 60s rest
Jump and pull knees to chest mid-air. Land soft. Builds vertical leap power critical for aerial striker duels.
E
Nordic Hamstring Curls 3×8 reps — SLOW and controlled
Kneel with feet anchored under something heavy, arms crossed, slowly lower your body toward floor using hamstrings. The #1 hamstring injury prevention exercise used by professional clubs worldwide. Essential for any player who sprints.

Advanced Mobility Protocol — 15 min

Couch Stretch (advanced hip flexor) 90 sec each side
Rear shin vertical on wall, front leg forward at 90°. This is the deepest hip flexor stretch. Unlocks maximum stride length and shooting power. Professional clubs use this daily. Push gently — don't arch your back.
Deep Squat Ankle Mobility 2 min continuous movement
Hold bottom of squat, shift weight side to side, rock forward on toes. Improves ankle dorsiflexion — critical for landing safely, planting to shoot, and changing direction without injury.
Thoracic Spine Rotation (with arm reach) 10 each side × 3
Half-kneeling, rotate upper body and reach toward ceiling. Improves upper body rotation for powerful shots and headers. Reduces lower back tension.
ITB / Lateral Hip Band Walk (or resistance-free) 15 steps each direction × 3
Slight squat, step laterally keeping toes forward. Activates glute medius — the muscle that stabilizes your knee. Weak glute medius = knee collapse when landing. This protects your knees from ACL risk.
PNF Hamstring Stretch 🦶 3 cycles each leg · hold LEFT 5 sec longer
Lie on back, partner holds leg up (or use wall). Tighten hamstring for 6 seconds pushing against resistance, then relax and push leg further. This is the most effective evidence-based stretching method. Each cycle gains 3-5° range of motion.
⚽ Afternoon · 55–60 min

STRIKER POSITIONING + LEFT FOOT FINISHING

World-class strikers know where to be before the ball arrives. That starts today.

The Striker Mindset
A striker's job is to score. Every movement, every touch, every decision is about creating or taking a goal opportunity. In your space, you'll simulate this mentally on every rep. Act as if a goalkeeper is in front of you. Pick your corner. Be deliberate.

Technical Warm-Up — 5 min

🦶 Left Foot Sole Roll + Burst 2 min
Roll ball, then suddenly accelerate 2 quick steps. Stop, reset. Simulates receiving and bursting into space. LEFT foot only.
Shooting Stance Drill — No ball 3 min
Practice the plant foot placement and follow-through motion 20 times each foot, no ball. Muscle memory for perfect shooting mechanics. Left foot: toes pointing at target, lean slightly forward, follow through across body.

Finishing Drills — 25 min 🦶

🦶 Left Foot Placed Shot (inside) vs wall 8 min · 60 shots
Plant foot beside ball, inside of left foot, aim for bottom corner (mark a spot on wall). This is a placed finish — not power. Like Olise's famous curling goals. Lock ankle, point toe down and out, smooth contact. Count accurate shots. Target: 70% accuracy by end of session.
🦶 Left Foot Power Shot (laces, top corner) 8 min · 50 shots
Same setup but strike with laces. Aim HIGH on the wall. Full follow-through, land on striking foot. This is how you score from 20 yards out. Build pace — first 20 shots at 70%, final 30 at full power. THIS is the left foot goal.
🦶 First Touch → Left Foot Finish 9 min
Throw ball against wall, control with left foot taking the ball away from wall (set yourself up), then shoot immediately. No second touches — first touch sets up, second touch finishes. This trains the striker's most important skill sequence.

Striker Movement Training — 15 min

Third Man Run Simulation 5 min
In your space, stand at one end. Sprint diagonally to opposite corner (simulate a run in behind). Touch wall, sprint back to original position. Repeat continuously. This trains the "run behind" movement pattern. Off the ball movement IS a skill.
🦶 Receive → Turn → Shoot (left foot) 5 min · 30 reps
Face away from wall. Throw ball over shoulder, turn, control with LEFT foot, shoot immediately. Simulates the striker turning their marker and finishing in one motion — the Kane/Lewandowski move, but with your weak foot.
Penalty Spot Ritual (left foot) 5 min · 20 penalties
Mark a spot 3m from wall. This is your penalty mark. Pre-kick routine: deep breath, pick your corner, don't look at the keeper (wall), step up, LEFT foot. Strike clean, follow through. Mental rehearsal + technical execution. Elite penalty takers visualize before every kick.

Weak Foot Juggling Goal — 5 min

🦶 Left Foot Juggling Max Count 5 min · beat your Day 1 record
Every session ends with left foot juggling. Write your max count. This number should be at least double your Day 1 record. If not — more reps tomorrow.
